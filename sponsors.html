Prewired.

An Edinburgh programming club for under 19s

Help us out?

Prewired is run by volunteers on a shoestring budget. Keeping young people enthusied about programming is really important, so if you'd like to help us keep going find out what we need.

What do we need?

Please email hello[at]prewired.org (or talk to Amy or Rikki at TMU Edinburgh - they'll mention Prewired in their intros) if you or the company you work for might be able to help us out with any of the following...

Equipment

Not all of the young people who come to Prewired can bring their own laptops. Currently some of our mentors lend their own out, but ideally we'd have a stash that attendees can borrow. Does your company have spare or old laptops lying around that could use a good home? Or could you buy us some cheap ones we can put Ubuntu on?

Catering

Every session we provide juice and light snacks for our attendees to keep them going and let them have a break. Currently volunteers take turns at buying these.

For upcoming special events - Prewired Maker Week in April and YRS in August - we'd like to provide light lunch daily.

Can you help us with a budget for this?

Expertise

If you're a company with employees to spare on Wednesday evenings, send them along to mentor!

Transport

We need to get a couple of dozen young people and mentors from Edinburgh to Plymouth and back for Young Rewired State in the last week of July. Sounds expensive. We'd like to be able to fund this for all of our attendees so nobody has to miss out for finanicial reasons. Can you help?

Prizes

We're holding a Prewired Maker Week in the Easter Holidays, and we want to give out small prizes for the projects the young people work on. Programming books? A fancy gaming mouse? Geeky tshirts? Suggestions and contributions are very welcome!

Work Experience

Many of our young people are at secondary school and have mandatory work experience coming up. These usually last one week. Some of the placements can be pretty rubbish. We think it would be really beneficial to be able to send them to a software company rather than something they're not really interested in. Can your company host an enthusiastic young coder for a week in spring/summer? We're building up a database so that we can pair young people with good placements when the time comes.

Organisers

Prewired is organised by Rikki Guy and Amy Guy, who can be reached via hello[at]prewired.org, and was founded by Ewan Klein, Freda O'Byrne, Amy Guy, Kit Barnes and Stuart Anderson and is supported by the University of Edinburgh School of Informatics.
